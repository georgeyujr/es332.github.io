U.P. MSI

and the Giant Clams of Bolinao, Pangasinan



02 SEPTEMBER 2016
Marine Ecosystem in Bolinao

The next itinerary which involved studying the concepts of Environmental Science was the U.P. Marine Science Instutute at Bolinao, Pangasinan.
We had a glimplse of the giant clams of Bolinao. It is also my first time to see a giant clam in person - that is, not just in pictures - and it really amazed me. The experience of encountering these giant clams firsthand is really an experience to remember.

However, on our trip to the site where the giant clams are, I've noticed dead milkfish floating in the sea. Our guide said there was a seasonal fish kill. This was not caused by factors created by man; rather, it was nature that did it. During this season, our guide said, the was a massive richness in the seasoil, which in turn caused the bloom of plant life in the sea, forcing the animals (which in this case, the milkfish) to compete for oxygen with these plants. This process is called eutrophication. Also, the phytoplanktons die quickly after consuming the plant in the seabeds. Their decay require oxygen in the process, again, forcing the animals in the sea to compete for oxygen, hence the death of the milkfish.

Firsthand encounter with Bolinao's giant clams is breathtaking. The giant clams are exceptionally beautiful. How I wish I had an underwater camera to take photos of them! I hope I will be able to go to Bolinao again and see these beautiful and extraordinary giant clams.
